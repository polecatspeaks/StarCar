## The classes this train named or confirmed

1. **A false premise outlives every gate until someone constructs the counterexample.**
   The shop-default carve-out ("environmental IO") passed spec review, plan review,
   and a faithful car - and died only when a reviewer BUILT the divergent record and
   ran both bodies. Reading is not verifying; constructing is the strongest verifying.
   And the escalation ladder above the conductor is load-bearing: the owner's up-level
   question ("why is budget-less even an option?") produced a better fix than either
   remedy on the table.
2. **Presenting the unbuilt as existing is one class across every surface** - it
   REJECTed the UI brief (a train presented as store data), the glossary (in-flight
   mechanisms in plain present), and now has a named instrument: the tense convention
   (plain present = runs today; RULED-in-flight marked). The commit-message tell:
   "the tense error is in the author's model, not a slip."
3. **Citations rot by self-invalidation** - a car's own earlier commit moved what its
   later commit cited. The durable form is section-anchored (JSON-pointer-style)
   citation, which survives the exact insertion that killed the line number. Fix the
   class, not the instance.
4. **An obligation satisfied at the artifact tier can fail at the visibility tier** -
   eleven issues existed and were invisible to the owner's board. Every
   decision-surface needs its own completeness check; never-drop is necessary, not
   sufficient.
5. **The board generates its own backlog** - first light produced three boarded
   issues with hypotheses and coordinates in its first minute. The instrument works;
   v0's discovery purpose is validated. IDLE joins the state vocabulary the way the
   detector was designed to force: loudly, with provenance.
6. **Mocks are direction, never contract** ("the track signal, not the track") - and
   the first live test of the doctrine worked: the mocks' amber-stale bent, the
   contract held, the conflict landed as feedback with a ruling.
